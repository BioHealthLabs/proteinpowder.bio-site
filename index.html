Protein 101 – What It Is and How It Works

Protein is essential for muscle growth, repair, and overall health. It helps repair damaged tissues and is a building block for bones, muscles, cartilage, skin, and blood.

In supplement form, protein powders are an easy and convenient way to boost your daily intake. The body absorbs different types of protein differently, making it important to choose the right type for your needs.

What protein powder can realistically do

  • Support muscle building and repair after workouts.
  • Improve recovery and reduce muscle soreness.
  • Help with fat loss when part of a controlled diet and exercise plan.
  • Enhance performance during physical activities.

What protein powder cannot do

  • Replace a balanced, healthy diet – protein should supplement, not replace whole foods.
  • Cause instant muscle gain – results take time and effort.
  • Work miracles without consistent training and nutrition.
  • Be a quick fix for weight loss or muscle building without a well-rounded fitness plan.

Protein Powder FAQ

Can I use protein powder daily?

Yes, protein powder can be used daily, but it’s important to not exceed your daily protein needs. It’s best to complement your protein powder intake with a balanced diet.

Is whey protein better than plant protein?

Whey protein is generally faster absorbed and contains all essential amino acids, making it ideal for muscle recovery. However, plant proteins can be just as effective for muscle gain and are ideal for vegans or those with dairy sensitivities.
